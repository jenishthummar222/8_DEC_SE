** Difference Between Broadband and Fiber-Optic Internet **

----------------------------------------------------------------------------
| Aspect       | Broadband Internet            | Fiber-Optic Internet      |
| ------------ | ----------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| Medium       | Copper cables                 | Fiber-optic cables        |
| Speed        | Moderate (up to 100â€“300 Mbps) | Very high (up to 1 Gbps+) |
| Latency      | Higher                        | Very low                  |
| Reliability  | Affected by interference      | Highly reliable           |
| Cost         | Cheaper                       | More expensive            |
| Availability | Widely available              | Limited in some areas     |
----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Explanation :- 

        Broadband is a general term for high-speed internet that is always on, commonly delivered via cable or DSL.

        Fiber-optic internet uses light signals through glass fibers, offering much faster and more stable connections.
